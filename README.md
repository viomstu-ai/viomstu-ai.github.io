# Privacy Policy for MorrowFernStudio Social Media Integrations

**Effective date: 22 July 2026**

## 1. Overview

This Privacy Policy explains how MorrowFernStudio’s social media integrations process information when connecting to social media platforms through their application programming interfaces, APIs, developer tools, OAuth systems, or other authorised integration methods.

The integrations are operated by MorrowFernStudio in Denmark.

The integrations are internal business tools used solely by the operator of MorrowFernStudio. They are not offered to customers, members of the public, or other social media users.

The integrations may connect to one or more social media platforms for purposes such as content publishing, account management, content management, analytics, reporting, automation, and maintaining authorised account connections.

## 3. Information accessed

The integrations only access information associated with social media accounts owned, operated, or expressly authorised by MorrowFernStudio.

Depending on the platform, integration, and permissions granted, the information accessed may include:

* Social media account identifiers
* Usernames, display names, profile information, and profile images
* Pages, profiles, channels, boards, groups, collections, or similar account structures
* Posts, Pins, videos, images, stories, reels, comments, captions, descriptions, links, and related metadata
* Media and content identifiers
* Publishing status and content-processing information
* Account, audience, engagement, reach, impression, conversion, or content-performance analytics
* Comments, messages, mentions, or other interactions, where the relevant permission has been granted
* Scheduling, publishing, and account-management information
* Technical information returned by the relevant platform API
* OAuth access tokens, refresh tokens, authorisation codes, and other credentials used to authenticate API requests
* Error messages, request logs, response logs, and other technical records needed to operate or troubleshoot the integrations

The integrations do not ordinarily allow members of the public or unrelated third parties to connect their social media accounts.

If this changes, this Privacy Policy will be updated before external users are permitted to use the integrations.

## 4. How information is used

Information accessed through social media APIs is used only to operate, manage, secure, and analyse MorrowFernStudio’s authorised social media accounts and internal publishing workflows.

This may include:

* Creating, uploading, scheduling, and publishing content
* Publishing images, videos, captions, descriptions, links, and other permitted media
* Selecting or managing pages, boards, channels, collections, albums, or similar destinations
* Updating or deleting published content where supported
* Retrieving information needed to confirm that content was processed or published successfully
* Managing drafts, publishing queues, and content schedules
* Reviewing account and content-performance data where the relevant permission has been granted
* Reading or managing comments, mentions, messages, or interactions where specifically authorised
* Preventing duplicate publication
* Maintaining and securing authorised connections with social media platforms
* Diagnosing errors and technical failures
* Maintaining records of internal automated actions
* Complying with platform rules, legal requirements, and security obligations

Information is not used for data brokerage, sale of personal information, advertising profiling of external individuals, or marketing to unrelated third parties.

## 5. Legal basis

Where the General Data Protection Regulation or other applicable data-protection law applies, information may be processed on the basis of:

* The legitimate interests of MorrowFernStudio in operating, maintaining, securing, and analysing its own social media accounts and internal publishing systems
* Performance of a contract, where processing is necessary to provide or maintain an authorised service
* Consent, where consent is required by law or by the relevant platform
* Compliance with a legal obligation
* Establishment, exercise, or defence of legal claims, where applicable

Because the integrations are used internally and ordinarily connect only to accounts operated by MorrowFernStudio, legitimate interests will normally be the primary legal basis.

Access to a social media account can generally be withdrawn by revoking the integration’s authorisation through the relevant social media platform, account settings, developer settings, or connected-app settings.

## 6. Storage and retention

OAuth access tokens, refresh tokens, API credentials, account identifiers, and related authentication information may be stored securely for as long as they are required to maintain an authorised connection.

Social media account and content information may be requested from the relevant platform when needed.

The integrations do not intentionally maintain a separate permanent database containing complete copies of social media account content unless storage is reasonably required for an authorised feature, such as:

* Content scheduling
* Publication records
* Duplicate-content prevention
* Analytics and performance reporting
* Error recovery
* Security monitoring
* Audit records
* Compliance with legal or platform obligations

Temporary information, publishing records, technical logs, API responses, error records, and security records may be retained for the limited period required to operate, secure, monitor, and troubleshoot the integrations.

Information will be deleted or anonymised when it is no longer required, when an integration is discontinued, when authorisation is revoked, or when deletion is otherwise required, subject to applicable legal, accounting, fraud-prevention, security, and record-keeping requirements.

Revoking access may prevent future API requests but may not automatically delete records that were lawfully created before access was revoked.

## 7. Sharing of information

Social media account information is not sold.

Information may be processed or disclosed to:

* The social media platforms that provide the relevant accounts, APIs, developer tools, and authentication services
* Hosting, cloud-storage, database, automation, or infrastructure providers used to operate the integrations
* Technical service providers used to secure, maintain, monitor, or troubleshoot the integrations
* Professional advisers where reasonably necessary
* Public authorities, regulators, courts, or law-enforcement bodies where disclosure is required by law
* A purchaser, successor, or relevant adviser in connection with a merger, restructuring, sale, or transfer of the business, subject to applicable legal safeguards

Service providers are only given access to information reasonably needed to provide their services.

Each social media platform processes information under its own privacy policy, developer terms, platform terms, and data-processing rules.

MorrowFernStudio does not control how a social media platform independently processes information within its own services.

## 10. Security

Reasonable technical and organisational measures are used to protect account information, API credentials, OAuth tokens, and integration data.

These measures may include:

* Restricting access to authorised persons and systems
* Using encrypted network connections
* Storing secrets separately from publicly accessible source code
* Using secure secret-management or environment-variable systems
* Limiting API permissions to those required for the integration’s functions
* Rotating or revoking credentials where necessary
* Monitoring publishing and authentication failures
* Maintaining technical and security logs
* Preventing unauthorised or duplicate publishing
* Reviewing integration access and permissions
* Applying security updates to relevant systems

No method of electronic storage, transmission, or authentication can guarantee complete security.

## 11. Automated processing

The integrations may use automated systems or software agents to prepare, schedule, manage, or publish content.

Automated processing may include:

* Selecting content from an approved publishing queue
* Formatting captions or descriptions
* Uploading approved media
* Publishing content at scheduled times
* Confirming publication status
* Recording content identifiers and publication results
* Retrieving authorised analytics
* Detecting technical failures or duplicate publication attempts

The integrations are not intended to make legal, employment, credit, insurance, healthcare, or similarly significant decisions about individuals.

Where appropriate, internal controls may be used to require human approval before particular actions are completed.
